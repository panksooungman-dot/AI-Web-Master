# Sequential Thinking MCP Integration

## Overview

The Sequential Thinking MCP server enables AI Business OS to solve complex problems through structured, step-by-step reasoning.

Rather than generating immediate conclusions, it decomposes problems into logical stages, evaluates alternatives, validates assumptions, and produces traceable decision processes.

---

# Purpose

Provide a standardized reasoning framework for solving complex technical, architectural, business, and operational challenges.

---

# Objectives

- Improve reasoning quality
- Reduce incorrect assumptions
- Increase decision transparency
- Support multi-step planning
- Enable traceable analysis
- Improve problem-solving consistency

---

# Capabilities

The Sequential Thinking MCP server supports:

- Problem decomposition

# Reasoning Principles

Every analysis should:

- Be logical
- Be sequential
- Be evidence-based
- Consider alternatives
- Identify dependencies
- Document assumptions

---

# Decision Framework

For every important decision, identify:

- Objective
- Constraints
- Assumptions
- Alternatives
- Benefits
- Risks
- Recommendation

---

# Assumption Policy

Always:

- Record assumptions.
- Validate assumptions when possible.
- Distinguish facts from hypotheses.
- Update assumptions when new evidence appears.

Never:

- Treat assumptions as confirmed facts.
- Ignore conflicting evidence.
- Hide uncertainty.

---

# Risk Assessment

Evaluate:

- Technical risks
- Business risks
- Security risks
- Operational risks
- Performance risks
- Cost risks

Each risk should include:

- Impact
- Likelihood
- Mitigation

---

# Validation Checklist

Before finalizing reasoning:

- Objectives are clear
- Context is sufficient
- Assumptions are documented
- Alternatives were evaluated
- Risks were assessed
- Recommendation is justified

---

# Best Practices

- Solve one problem at a time.
- Break large problems into smaller tasks.
- Reuse previous decisions when appropriate.
- Keep reasoning transparent.
- Document conclusions clearly.
- Update Decision Memory after major decisions.

---

# Success Criteria

Sequential Thinking is successful when:

- Problems are fully understood.
- Decisions are well justified.
- Alternatives are considered.
- Risks are documented.
- Reasoning is reproducible.
- Outcomes are traceable.
