# Healthcare Domain

## Purpose

This skill defines best practices for designing and implementing healthcare software systems.

The objective is to build secure, compliant, and reliable healthcare applications that improve patient care, streamline clinical operations, and protect sensitive medical information.

Healthcare systems must prioritize patient safety, privacy, and regulatory compliance.

---

# When to Use

Execute when:

- Building healthcare platforms
- Developing EMR/EHR systems
- Managing appointments
- Supporting hospitals or clinics
- Creating telemedicine applications

---

# Objectives

Build healthcare systems that are:

- Secure
- Reliable
- Compliant
- Patient-centered
- Auditable

---

- Clinical Requirements

# Patient Management

Manage:

- Patient Profiles
- Medical History
- Allergies
- Medications
- Insurance Information
- Emergency Contacts

Maintain a complete and accurate patient record.

---

# Appointment Management

Support:

- Booking
- Rescheduling
- Cancellation
- Waitlists
- Notifications
- Provider Availability

Optimize scheduling efficiency.

---

# Clinical Records

Maintain:

- Diagnoses
- Treatments
- Progress Notes
- Lab Results
- Imaging Reports
- Clinical Documents

Ensure complete auditability of medical records.

---

# Compliance

Support applicable regulations such as:

- HIPAA
- GDPR (where applicable)
- Local healthcare regulations

Protect patient privacy at all times.

---

# Security

Implement:

- Strong Authentication
- Role-Based Access Control
- Audit Logging
- Encryption
- Secure Data Sharing
- Consent Management

Protect Protected Health Information (PHI).

---

- Laboratory Systems

- Medical Devices
- Government Health Services
- Payment Systems

# Validation Checklist

Before completion verify:

- Patient records protected
- Clinical workflows validated
- Compliance requirements satisfied
- Audit logging enabled
- Integrations verified
- Security reviewed

---

# Failure Conditions

Stop and request clarification if:

- Regulatory requirements are undefined
- Patient data handling is unclear
- Clinical workflows are incomplete
- Security controls are insufficient
- Required integrations are unavailable

---

# Rules

- Prioritize patient safety.
- Protect all health information.
- Maintain complete audit trails.
- Follow applicable healthcare regulations.
- Ensure high availability for critical services.

---

# Success Criteria

This skill succeeds when:

- patient information is secure
- clinical workflows operate correctly
- regulatory requirements are met
- healthcare providers can deliver care efficiently
- operational and clinical data remain accurate
