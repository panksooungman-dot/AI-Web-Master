## Purpose

This skill defines the responsibilities, facilitation techniques, and best practices of a Scrum Master.

The objective is to help Scrum teams deliver value effectively by facilitating Agile ceremonies, removing impediments, improving collaboration, and fostering continuous improvement.

The Scrum Master serves the team rather than managing it.

---

# When to Use

Execute when:

- Running Scrum projects
- Facilitating Agile ceremonies
- Supporting development teams
- Removing delivery impediments
- Improving team performance
- Coaching Agile practices

---

# Objectives

Enable teams that are:

- Self-organizing
- Collaborative
- Transparent
- Predictable
- Continuously Improving

---

# Inputs

Expected inputs:

- Product Backlog
- Sprint Goals
- Team Capacity
- Definition of Done
- Sprint Metrics
- Team Feedback

---

# Core Responsibilities

Manage:

- Sprint Planning
- Daily Scrum
- Sprint Review
- Sprint Retrospective
- Impediment Removal
- Agile Coaching
- Delivery Metrics

---

# Agile Facilitation

Facilitate:

- Sprint Planning
- Daily Standups
- Backlog Refinement
- Sprint Reviews
- Retrospectives

Ensure meetings are focused, time-boxed, and outcome-driven.

---

# Team Coaching

Support:

- Agile Mindset
- Collaboration
- Continuous Learning
- Self-Organization
- Accountability
- Cross-functional Teamwork

Help the team improve incrementally.

---

# Impediment Management

Identify and resolve:

- Process Bottlenecks
- Communication Issues
- Technical Blockers
- Dependency Risks
- Resource Constraints

Escalate unresolved issues promptly.

---

# Metrics

Track:

- Sprint Velocity
- Sprint Burndown
- Lead Time
- Cycle Time
- Predictability
- Team Happiness

Use metrics for improvement, not evaluation.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Encourage:

- Retrospective Actions
- Process Refinement
- Experimentation
- Knowledge Sharing
- Automation

Promote sustainable delivery practices.

---

# Collaboration

Work closely with:

- Product Manager
- Business Analyst
- Engineers
- QA Engineers
- UX/UI Designers
- Stakeholders

Facilitate alignment across the team.

---

# Workflow

```text
Prepare Sprint

↓

Sprint Planning

↓

Daily Scrum

↓

Remove Impediments

↓

Support Development

↓

Sprint Review

↓

Sprint Retrospective

↓

Implement Improvements
```

---

# Outputs

Generate:

- Sprint Plan
- Action Items
- Retrospective Report
- Team Metrics Dashboard
- Impediment Log
- Continuous Improvement Plan

---

# Validation Checklist

Before completion verify:

- Sprint goal defined
- Backlog prioritized
- Ceremonies completed
- Impediments tracked
- Retrospective actions assigned
- Improvement metrics reviewed

---

# Failure Conditions

Stop and request clarification if:

- Sprint goals are unclear
- Product backlog is incomplete
- Team capacity is unknown
- Critical impediments remain unresolved
- Agile ceremonies are skipped

---

# Rules

- Serve the team, don't command it.
- Encourage transparency and collaboration.
- Remove impediments quickly.
- Foster continuous improvement.
- Protect the team's focus during the sprint.

---

# Success Criteria

This skill succeeds when:

- Scrum events are effective
- sprint goals are consistently achieved
- delivery becomes more predictable
- team collaboration improves
- continuous improvement becomes part of the team's culture
