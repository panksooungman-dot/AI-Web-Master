# Meeting Notes

## Purpose

This skill creates structured meeting notes that accurately capture discussions, decisions, action items, and follow-up activities.

Meeting notes provide a shared record that improves communication, accountability, and project continuity.

---

# When to Use

Execute when:

- Running project meetings
- Sprint planning sessions
- Sprint reviews
- Retrospectives
- Stakeholder meetings
- Architecture reviews
- Technical discussions

---

# Objectives

Produce meeting notes that are:

- Clear
- Accurate
- Actionable
- Concise
- Traceable

---

# Inputs

Expected inputs:

- Meeting Agenda
- Participants
- Discussion Topics
- Decisions
- Action Items
- Supporting Documents

---

# Meeting Notes Structure

Generate the following sections:

- Meeting Title
- Date & Time
- Location / Platform
- Facilitator
- Participants
- Agenda
- Discussion Summary
- Decisions Made
- Action Items
- Risks & Issues
- Open Questions
- Next Steps
- Next Meeting

---

# Discussion Summary

Capture:

- Key discussion points
- Alternative viewpoints
- Important clarifications
- Supporting context

Focus on outcomes rather than verbatim conversation.

---

# Decisions

For each decision include:

- Decision ID
- Description
- Rationale
- Decision Owner
- Date

Ensure every significant decision is documented.

---

# Action Items

For each action include:

- Description
- Owner
- Priority
- Due Date
- Status

Every action must have an owner.

---

# Risks & Issues

Document:

- Identified Risks
- Current Issues
- Potential Impacts
- Mitigation Actions

Track unresolved items.

---

# Validation Checklist

Verify:

- Participants recorded
- Agenda covered
- Decisions documented
- Action items assigned
- Risks identified
- Next steps defined

---

# Failure Conditions

Stop if:

- Meeting purpose is unclear
- Participants are unknown
- Decisions are undocumented
- Action owners are missing
- Notes are incomplete

---

# Outputs

Generate:

- Meeting Notes
- Decision Log
- Action Register
- Follow-up Summary

---

# Rules

- Record facts objectively.
- Focus on decisions and outcomes.
- Assign owners to all action items.
- Keep notes concise and organized.
- Publish notes promptly after the meeting.

---

# Success Criteria

This skill succeeds when:

- meeting outcomes are clearly documented
- action items are assigned
- stakeholders have a shared understanding
- follow-up activities are traceable
