# Product Requirements Document (PRD)

## Purpose

This skill creates a comprehensive Product Requirements Document (PRD) that aligns business goals, user needs, and engineering execution.

The PRD serves as the primary source of truth for product development and ensures all stakeholders share a common understanding of the product before implementation begins.

---

# When to Use

Execute when:

- Starting a new product
- Planning a new feature
- Defining an MVP
- Expanding an existing product
- Aligning cross-functional teams
- Preparing engineering work

---

# Objectives

Produce a PRD that is:

- Clear
- Complete
- Actionable
- Testable
- Traceable
- Measurable

---

# Inputs

Expected inputs:

- Business Requirements
- Product Vision
- User Research
- Market Research
- Stakeholder Feedback
- Technical Constraints

---

# PRD Structure

Generate the following sections:

- Executive Summary
- Product Vision
- Business Objectives
- Problem Statement
- Target Users
- Personas
- User Journey
- Scope
- Out of Scope
- Functional Requirements
- Non-functional Requirements
- User Stories
- Assumptions
- Constraints
- Dependencies
- Risks
- Success Metrics
- Milestones
- Acceptance Criteria

---

# Product Vision

Describe:

- Product Purpose
- Business Value
- Customer Value
- Competitive Advantage
- Long-term Vision

The vision should guide every requirement.

---

# Functional Requirements

Document:

- Core Features
- Business Rules
- User Flows
- Edge Cases
- Integrations
- Permission Rules

Assign a unique identifier to every requirement.

---

# Non-functional Requirements

Specify:

- Performance
- Scalability
- Reliability
- Availability
- Security
- Accessibility
- Maintainability
- Compliance

Ensure every quality attribute is measurable.

---

# User Stories

For each major feature include:

- User Role
- User Goal
- Business Value
- Acceptance Criteria

Follow the standard User Story format.

---

# Success Metrics

Define measurable KPIs such as:

- User Adoption
- Active Users
- Customer Satisfaction
- Retention
- Conversion Rate
- Revenue Impact
- Performance Targets

Every objective should have at least one measurable outcome.

---

# Risks

Identify:

- Business Risks
- Technical Risks
- Operational Risks
- Schedule Risks
- Resource Risks

Document mitigation strategies for each risk.

---

# Acceptance Criteria

Each feature should define:

- Preconditions
- Expected Behavior
- Validation Method
- Completion Criteria

Acceptance criteria should be objective and testable.

---

# Validation Checklist

Before completion verify:

- Product vision documented
- Objectives clearly defined
- Scope approved
- Functional requirements complete
- Non-functional requirements measurable
- User stories included
- Success metrics defined
- Risks documented
- Acceptance criteria complete
- Stakeholder review completed

---

# Failure Conditions

Stop and request clarification if:

- Product vision is unclear
- Business objectives are undefined
- Requirements are incomplete
- Scope is ambiguous
- Success metrics cannot be measured
- Stakeholder alignment is missing

---

# Outputs

Generate:

- Product Requirements Document
- Functional Requirement List
- Non-functional Requirement List
- User Story Catalog
- Acceptance Criteria
- Success Metrics Dashboard

---

# Rules

- Focus on business value rather than implementation.
- Keep requirements clear and unambiguous.
- Ensure every requirement is testable.
- Separate requirements from technical design.
- Maintain traceability from business goals to implementation.

---

# Success Criteria

This skill succeeds when:

- stakeholders agree on product direction
- requirements are complete and testable
- engineering can begin without ambiguity
- product goals are measurable
- business value is clearly defined
